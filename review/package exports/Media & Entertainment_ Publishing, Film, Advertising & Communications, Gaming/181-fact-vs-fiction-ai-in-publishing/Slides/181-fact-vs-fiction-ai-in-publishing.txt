Title: Defining the Core Problem

Subtitle: When AI facts go unchecked

Content:
• AI tools can generate confident, plausible-sounding facts that are simply wrong
• No built-in fact-checking
• Publishers who rely on unverified AI output risk spreading misinformation to large audiences
• Errors appear polished and credible, making them harder to spot
• The problem grows when content moves quickly through editorial pipelines without review

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Spotting AI-Generated Misinformation

Subtitle: Recognising the signs before publishing

Content:
• Vague, unverifiable statistics cited
• Confident tone, thin evidence
• No named sources given
• Dates and facts conflict
• Generic quotes, no attribution

Slide 7 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Spotting AI Errors in Practice

Subtitle: Recognising mistakes before they go live

Content:
• AI-generated text often includes plausible-sounding statistics with no verifiable source
• Dates and names are common failure points
• Watch for quotes attributed to real people that sound slightly off or uncharacteristically vague
• Cross-referencing a single claim against two or three reliable sources catches most errors quickly
• Confident tone is not proof of accuracy
