Title: False Positives vs False Negatives

Subtitle: The real cost of getting it wrong.

Content:
• Two errors, opposite costs
• False positive: good rejected
• False negative: bad accepted
• Both hurt your business
• Getting it wrong is expensive

Slide 3 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Cost of Getting It Wrong

Subtitle: Mistakes carry a measurable price tag.

Content:
• Every misclassification in a production line has a direct financial consequence
• False positives waste good product
• Scrapping or reworking items that were never actually faulty adds up fast
• False negatives let defective goods reach customers, triggering recalls, complaints, and lost trust
• The "safer" error depends entirely on your industry and what failure actually costs

Slide 5 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Calculating the True Cost

Subtitle: Beyond the obvious financial impact

Content:
• Every false positive wastes materials, labour, and machine time on a product that was never actually faulty
• Reputation damage when customers lose trust in your quality process
• False negatives carry liability risk
• Rework and reinspection cycles compound costs quickly across high-volume production lines
• Hidden overhead from over-inspection, including staff time spent reviewing flagged items that pass on second check

Slide 7 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Balancing Errors in Practice

Subtitle: Striking the right balance every time

Content:
• Setting a threshold too high catches more defects but flags more good parts as faulty
• Costs cut both ways
• Every adjustment to sensitivity shifts the balance between missed faults and wasted product
• Regular calibration using real production data keeps the model tuned to your actual environment
• Cross-functional teams (quality, operations, and engineering) should review error rates together
• Small threshold changes can have a surprisingly large impact on overall yield and customer satisfaction

Slide 8 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Points to Remember

Subtitle: Core principles to guide your decisions

Content:
• Both errors cost money
• Context shapes the right balance
• Neither zero is realistic
• Know your product's risk level
• Measure, then decide
• Start small, adjust often
