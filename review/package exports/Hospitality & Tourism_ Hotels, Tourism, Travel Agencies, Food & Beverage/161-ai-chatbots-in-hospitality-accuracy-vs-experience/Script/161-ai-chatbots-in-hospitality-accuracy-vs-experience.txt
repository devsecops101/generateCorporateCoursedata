So, how do we actually reduce the risk of chatbot errors causing real problems for guests or the business? The key is building in verification at every critical step. Before any booking is confirmed, always double-check the important details, things like dates, room type, and guest numbers, against your live reservation system rather than trusting the chatbot alone. If a guest's request is unclear or unusual, route it to a human member of staff straight away. It is also worth training your team to recognise the kinds of mistakes chatbots commonly make, such as misreading dates or creating duplicate bookings. Keeping a clear record of chatbot conversations means that if something does go wrong, you can trace it quickly and put it right. And for high-value or complicated reservations, always have a person involved in the final decision. Think of verification not as extra work, but as your safety net.
