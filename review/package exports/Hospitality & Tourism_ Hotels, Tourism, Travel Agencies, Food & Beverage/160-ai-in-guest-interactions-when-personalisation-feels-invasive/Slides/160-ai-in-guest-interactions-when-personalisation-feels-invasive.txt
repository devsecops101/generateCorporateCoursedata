Title: Welcome: Personalisation vs Privacy

Subtitle: Where does helpful end and intrusive begin?

Content:
• AI can remember guest preferences, past stays, and personal details to shape every interaction
• This creates powerful, tailored experiences
• But guests don't always know what data is being used or how
• Personalisation can quickly shift from feeling helpful to feeling watched
• This lesson explores where that line sits and how to stay on the right side of it

Slide 3 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Personalisation Paradox Explained

Subtitle: When comfort meets concern for guests.

Content:
• Guests want personalised service but often feel uneasy when hotels know too much
• The line between helpful and intrusive is surprisingly thin
• A warm welcome using a guest's name feels different from referencing their dietary history unprompted
• Data collection powers personalisation
• What delights one guest can unsettle another, depending on their expectations and past experiences
• Comfort and concern can exist at the same time

Slide 4 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Consent and Transparency Line

Subtitle: Where openness builds or breaks trust

Content:
• Ask before you collect
• Be clear, not clever
• Silence isn't consent
• Explain what data does
• Opt-out must be easy

Slide 5 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Reading the Room: Guest Signals

Subtitle: Spotting comfort and discomfort in guests

Content:
• Guests who freely share preferences are signalling openness to personalisation
• Hesitation, short answers, or topic changes often indicate discomfort
• Watch body language too
• Repeat visits with consistent behaviour suggest a guest is comfortable with how their information is used
• A guest who seems surprised by what you know may not have expected that level of detail

Title: The Opt-In Advantage

Subtitle: Consent builds trust and loyalty naturally

Content:
• Guests choose what to share
• Consent reduces privacy complaints
• Opt-in data is more accurate
• Trust grows from transparency
• Loyalty follows genuine choice

Slide 7 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Line Worth Not Crossing

Subtitle: Where trust ends and discomfort begins.

Content:
• Guests notice when AI knows things they never shared directly
• Inferring preferences from behaviour feels clever until it feels creepy
• The tipping point: when personalisation signals surveillance rather than care
• Relevance matters
• Timing, tone, and context all shape whether a suggestion feels helpful or intrusive
• A single misjudged interaction can erode trust built over many visits
