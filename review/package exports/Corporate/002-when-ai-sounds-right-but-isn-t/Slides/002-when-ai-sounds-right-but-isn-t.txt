Title: When AI Sounds Plausible

Subtitle: Confidence can mask hidden errors

Content:
• AI tools often produce responses that sound authoritative even when details are subtly wrong
• Fluent, confident language is not the same as accurate language
• Small errors
• Mistakes can appear in facts, figures, dates, and named sources
• The more polished the output, the easier it is to trust without checking

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Spotting the Subtle Warning Signs

Subtitle: Clues hiding in plain sight

Content:
• Vague sources, no specifics
• Overly confident tone
• Suspiciously perfect answers
• Details that almost fit
• Plausible but unverifiable claims

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Plausible Errors Hide in Plain Sight

Subtitle: Confident tone masks hidden mistakes

Content:
• AI outputs often sound authoritative even when the underlying facts are wrong
• Fluent, well-structured sentences signal confidence, not accuracy
• Small errors (a wrong date, a misattributed quote) blend into otherwise correct text
• No warning signs appear
• Plausible-sounding details are the hardest mistakes to catch because they feel familiar
• Readers naturally trust polished prose

Slide 7 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Fact-Check Before You Trust

Subtitle: Simple steps to verify AI output.

Content:
• Cross-check with trusted sources
• Search the original claim
• Check dates and context
• Ask who said it
• Verify numbers independently

Slide 8 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What to Remember

Subtitle: Points to carry forward with confidence

Content:
• AI can be confidently wrong, not just obviously wrong
• Subtle inaccuracies often hide in plausible-sounding details like dates, names, and statistics
• Always verify claims that matter
• Your critical eye is the most reliable filter you have
• Cross-checking with trusted sources takes seconds and prevents costly mistakes
• Start small by fact-checking one AI output this week
