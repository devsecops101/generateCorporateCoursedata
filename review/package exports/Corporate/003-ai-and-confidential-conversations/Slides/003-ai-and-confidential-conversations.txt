Slide 3 (Slide 2)

Title: What Gets Shared With AI

Subtitle: More than you might realise

Content:
• Names of colleagues
• Meeting topics and dates
• Internal project details
• Personal or HR matters
• Financial figures and targets
• Client and customer data

Slide 4 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What Gets Captured by AI

Subtitle: More than you might expect

Content:
• Spoken words, names, and job titles mentioned during AI-assisted meetings
• Sensitive topics raised in passing, even briefly
• Full chat transcripts, not just summaries
• Document contents shared or pasted into AI tools
• Emotional tone and context inferred from your language

Slide 5 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What You Should Avoid

Subtitle: Sharing sensitive details with AI tools

Content:
• Client names or details
• Passwords or login credentials
• Confidential meeting notes
• Personal staff information
• Unreleased business plans

Slide 6 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What to Share With AI

Subtitle: Think before you type or speak.

Content:
• General questions and publicly available information
• Anonymised examples with all names and identifiers removed
• Your own ideas and drafts that contain no client or colleague details
• Task prompts that are specific but not sensitive
• Nothing you would hesitate to post on a public noticeboard

Slide 7 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What You Should Do Next

Subtitle: Practical steps to protect confidential information

Content:
• Check before you speak, not after
• Treat AI tools like a public microphone in a private room
• Remove or anonymise names, client details, and sensitive figures before inputting anything
• Ask your manager or IT team which AI tools are approved for work use
• Report any accidental sharing of confidential data to your line manager straight away
• Build a quick mental habit: pause, review, then proceed

Slide 8 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What to Remember

Subtitle: Keep these principles close at hand.

Content:
• Never paste confidential information into a public or unverified AI tool
• Assume anything you type could be stored or reviewed
• Check your organisation's policy before using AI in any work context
• When in doubt, leave it out
• Treat AI tools with the same caution you would apply to a public forum
• Start by reviewing your workplace's AI usage guidelines this week
