Title: Using AI with Customer Data

Subtitle: Practical guardrails for every interaction.

Content:
• AI tools are everywhere in modern workplaces
• Customer data requires careful handling at every step
• Small mistakes can lead to serious privacy breaches
• This course gives you practical, easy-to-follow guardrails
• No technical background needed

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Why This Matters Now

Subtitle: AI use is growing fast, act now.

Content:
• AI tools are now embedded in everyday business software, from email to spreadsheets
• Staff are using them whether policies exist or not
• Customer data is being shared with these tools, often without realising it
• Regulators are paying close attention
• The risks are real and the time to act is now

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Recognising Sensitive Customer Data

Subtitle: What counts as sensitive information

Content:
• Names, addresses, phone numbers
• Payment and banking details
• Health or medical records
• Login credentials and passwords
• Purchase history and preferences

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Anonymisation Process

Subtitle: Strip identifiers before sharing.

Content:
• Remove names first
• Delete contact details
• Swap IDs for codes
• Check dates and locations
• Then share safely

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Your Daily Checklist

Subtitle: Practical steps to protect customer data

Content:
• Use fictional examples when testing AI features
• Check your organisation's policy before sharing data
• Treat AI input fields like public spaces
• Keep a record of data types used with AI tools
• Apply the minimum data needed principle

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Building Safe Workflows

Subtitle: Creating repeatable data protection habits.

Content:
• Develop a standard cleaning routine before AI use
• Use placeholder codes consistently
• Double-check for overlooked identifiers
• Test your anonymisation with sample data first
• Make data protection automatic, not optional

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Red Flags to Watch For

Subtitle: Warning signs that demand your attention

Content:
• AI requests full customer databases
• Output includes real names unexpectedly
• No opt-out option offered
• Tool stores your inputs automatically
• Vague privacy policy or none

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What to Remember

Subtitle: Keep these principles close at hand.

Content:
• Never paste real customer data into unverified AI tools
• Anonymise first, then prompt
• Always check your organisation's policy
• If in doubt, leave it out
• Start small, review results, build confidence gradually
