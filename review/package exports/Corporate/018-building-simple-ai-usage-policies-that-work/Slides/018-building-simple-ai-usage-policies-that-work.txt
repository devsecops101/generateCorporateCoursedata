Slide 1 (Slide 1)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Why AI Policies End Up Ignored

Subtitle: Five common structural problems

Content:
• Too long to read (20+ pages)
• Too vague to apply in practice
• No staff training provided
• Outdated when tools evolve
• No clear ownership

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Root Problem with Failed Policies

Subtitle: Written for lawyers, not workers

Content:
• Created by legal/IT without user input
• Abstract guidance with no real examples
• Built around fear rather than practical use
• Disconnected from actual workflows
• Lands in inbox, gets skimmed once, forgotten

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: How Poor Design Kills Adoption

Subtitle: When policies ignore reality

Content:
• Legal jargon instead of plain language
• No connection to daily work tasks
• Staff excluded from creation process
• Zero practical scenarios provided
• Feels punitive, not helpful

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Essential Components of Working Policies

Subtitle: Six elements that ensure compliance

Content:
• Specific approved AI tools listed
• Concrete task examples (allowed/prohibited)
• Guidelines for sensitive information
• Named policy owner for questions
• Scheduled review dates
• Plain language throughout

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Framework for Effective Policy Structure

Subtitle: Clear purpose drives better outcomes

Content:
• Defined scope and purpose statement
• Specific approved use cases
• Clear boundaries and restrictions
• Regular update expectations
• Simple, conversational tone

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Power of Plain Language

Subtitle: Simplicity reduces errors and improves compliance

Content:
• One rule per sentence
• No jargon or acronyms
• Fits on single page
• Written for actual users
• Clear expectations, fewer mistakes

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Practical Implementation Checklist

Subtitle: What your policy needs to function

Content:
• 5-minute reading time maximum
• Real examples for common situations
• Sensitive data handling protocols
• Designated point of contact
• Built-in revision schedule
• User-friendly language

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Principles and Next Steps

Subtitle: Start small, involve people, iterate

Content:
• Simple beats comprehensive every time
• Focus on behaviors, not just tools
• Include your team in creation
• Start with one use case
• Action item: Draft 3 rules for one AI scenario this week
