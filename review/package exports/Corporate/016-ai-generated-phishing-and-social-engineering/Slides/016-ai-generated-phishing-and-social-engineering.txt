Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What Is AI Phishing?

Subtitle: How machines create convincing scams

Content:
• Phishing attacks powered by artificial intelligence
• AI writes, personalises, and scales scam messages
• Uses your online data to craft realistic content
• Delivered via email, text, calls, or social media
• Far more sophisticated than traditional template scams

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: AI's Personalisation Advantage

Subtitle: Why these scams feel so convincing

Content:
• AI analyses your name, job title, and online activity
• Creates messages that feel genuinely personal
• References recent purchases or interactions
• Mimics writing style of trusted contacts
• Builds false sense of legitimacy and familiarity

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Red Flags in AI Messages

Subtitle: Warning signs that reveal the scam

Content:
• Urgent requests for immediate action
• Suspiciously perfect grammar and writing
• Unexpected links or attachments
• Pressure without time to verify
• Tone feels familiar but slightly off

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Technical Warning Signs

Subtitle: What to check before you click

Content:
• Sender addresses with subtle differences
• Generic greetings despite personal details
• Requests for passwords or login credentials
• Links that don't match expected destinations
• Messages arriving after recent online activity

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Common Attack Scenarios

Subtitle: How AI phishing typically unfolds

Content:
• Fake urgent account closure warnings
• Impersonated managers requesting transfers
• False IT security alerts needing login
• Bogus invoice confirmations with attachments
• Phoney customer service follow-ups

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Staying Safe From AI Scams

Subtitle: Practical steps to protect yourself

Content:
• Pause before clicking any links
• Verify senders through independent channels
• Use multi-factor authentication everywhere
• Trust your instincts when something feels wrong
• Report suspicious messages to protect others

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Takeaways

Subtitle: Remember these essential points

Content:
• AI makes scams highly personal and polished
• Urgency is the attacker's primary weapon
• Always verify unexpected requests separately
• No legitimate organisation demands instant action
• Your instincts are your first line of defence
