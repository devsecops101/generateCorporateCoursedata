Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Reality Behind "AI-Powered"

Subtitle: What vendors actually mean by their buzzwords

Content:
• "AI-powered" often means basic automation or keyword matching
• Vendors rarely specify which type of AI they actually use
• Terms like "intelligent" and "smart" are marketing language, not technical descriptions
• No industry standard defines what counts as AI in a product
• The boldest claims tend to appear where evidence is thinnest

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: How Marketing Shapes AI Claims

Subtitle: Why vague language dominates vendor pitches

Content:
• Marketing teams drive the wording, not technical teams
• Buzzwords replace precise technical explanations
• Vague claims invite customers to fill gaps with assumptions
• "Next-generation" and similar phrases sound impressive but mean nothing
• Lack of standard definitions allows anyone to use AI labels freely

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Verifying AI Performance Claims

Subtitle: Independent testing and reliable benchmarks

Content:
• Run standardised benchmark tests for consistent comparison
• Check independent audit results from unbiased third parties
• Look for reproducible performance data, not just vendor figures
• Seek credible reviews from researchers or industry analysts
• Compare vendor claims directly against available evidence

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Essential Questions for Vendors

Subtitle: What to ask during sales conversations

Content:
• Request live demos with real data, not polished videos
• Ask for specific accuracy figures and how they were measured
• Find out if the AI was trained on data relevant to your industry
• Clarify who is responsible when the system makes errors
• Determine whether human review is still required at any stage

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Major Red Flags in AI Claims

Subtitle: Warning signs that demand immediate scrutiny

Content:
• Claims of "100% accuracy" or "fully autonomous" operation
• No disclosed error rates or system limitations
• Inability to explain what the AI actually does
• Zero real-world examples or independent testing
• Pressure to decide quickly without proper evaluation
• No clear accountability when errors occur

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Recognising Misleading Sales Tactics

Subtitle: Common patterns in oversold AI products

Content:
• Cherry-picked case studies showing only best outcomes
• Guaranteed results promised despite AI's inherent uncertainty
• Evasive answers when asked about failure scenarios
• Heavy reliance on impressive-sounding but meaningless terminology
• Reluctance to provide technical details or testing methodology
• Claims that seem too good to be true for the price point

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Your Evaluation Framework

Subtitle: Core principles for smart AI procurement

Content:
• Always demand real evidence, not marketing materials
• Test systems in your own environment before committing
• Treat vague claims as red flags requiring deeper investigation
• Push for specific, measurable results with clear methodology
• Maintain healthy skepticism throughout the evaluation process
