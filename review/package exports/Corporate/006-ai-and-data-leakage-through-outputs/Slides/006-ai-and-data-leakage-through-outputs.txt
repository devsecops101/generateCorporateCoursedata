Title: What Is AI Data Leakage?

Subtitle: When AI reveals what it should not

Content:
• AI systems can accidentally reveal sensitive information in their responses
• This happens when private data used to train or prompt the AI surfaces unexpectedly
• Examples include names, medical details, financial records, or internal business data appearing in outputs
• Users often unaware the information was ever stored or accessible
• Not always caused by hacking, sometimes just a poorly worded question is enough

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Why Data Leakage Matters

Subtitle: The hidden reach of AI outputs

Content:
• AI outputs are often logged and stored by systems
• Responses can be read by administrators, third-party providers, or other users
• What appears as helpful answers may contain hidden sensitive information
• Most users cannot spot when leakage has occurred
• The risk extends beyond what you see on your screen

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Two Main Sources of Leakage

Subtitle: Training data and user inputs combined

Content:
• Training datasets: AI systems learn from vast amounts of real-world data
• User inputs: Sensitive details fed directly into the system during use
• Repeated or patterned inputs increase exposure risk
• No deliberate intent required for a leak to occur
• Both sources can combine to create unexpected revelations

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: How Training Data Creates Risk

Subtitle: What AI learns can resurface later

Content:
• Large datasets may absorb sensitive information without deliberate intent
• Personal details, internal documents, or confidential records can reappear
• No automatic filter scrubs sensitive content before training
• Even anonymised data carries risk if patterns allow identification
• Information doesn't disappear after training completes

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Common Leakage Scenarios

Subtitle: Everyday situations where data slips through

Content:
• Chatbot repeats user's salary details from earlier conversation
• AI recalls and surfaces colleague's medical condition inappropriately
• System exposes confidential client names in suggestions
• Output accidentally includes private login credentials
• AI reveals restricted project data in responses

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Industry Examples of Leakage

Subtitle: Real incidents across different sectors

Content:
• Writing tools suggest colleague names in documents
• Email autocomplete exposes previous recipients inappropriately
• Generated reports include unintended salary or budget figures
• Healthcare AI summaries surface patient medical details
• Coding assistants include real passwords in suggested snippets

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Prevention Strategies

Subtitle: Practical steps to protect sensitive information

Content:
• Avoid pasting real names and personal details into prompts
• Use dummy or synthetic data for testing purposes
• Review all outputs carefully before sharing with others
• Keep prompts general rather than including sensitive specifics
• Stay alert for unexpected or out-of-place information in responses

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Principles to Remember

Subtitle: Building safe AI habits

Content:
• Pause before pasting: consider if information is sensitive
• Treat AI outputs like shareable emails that others might see
• When in doubt about sensitivity, leave it out
• Your organisation's data policies apply to AI tools too
• Review one recent AI interaction this week to identify missed risks
