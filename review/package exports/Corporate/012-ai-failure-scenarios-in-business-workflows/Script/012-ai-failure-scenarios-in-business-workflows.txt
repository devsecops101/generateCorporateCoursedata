Welcome, and thank you for joining this course. Over the next few lessons, we're going to look at something that doesn't get talked about nearly enough: what happens when AI goes wrong inside a real business. And the honest answer is, it happens more often than most people realise. When AI tools fail in a workflow, the knock-on effects can be significant. We're talking about lost time, frustrated teams, and real financial costs. Now, the good news is that you don't need to be technical to understand any of this. This course has been designed for anyone who works alongside AI tools, regardless of your background. We'll be drawing on examples from actual businesses, so everything we cover is grounded in reality rather than theory. By the end, you'll have a much clearer sense of where the risks tend to show up and how to spot them before they cause serious problems.

Script 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Let me paint a picture for you. Imagine it's a busy Monday morning, and your business relies on an automated chatbot to handle customer enquiries and process orders. Everything looks fine, until suddenly, the chatbot goes silent. Customers are reaching out, but nobody is responding. Meanwhile, orders are piling up in a queue with nothing to process them. Your staff, who were never really trained for this situation, are now scrambling to figure out how to handle everything manually. They're digging through old spreadsheets, making phone calls, and essentially rebuilding processes from scratch under pressure. And here's the really uncomfortable part: all of this can happen within just a few hours, and by that point, you're already losing revenue and dealing with angry customers. This isn't a worst-case fantasy—this is the kind of disruption that businesses genuinely face when customer-facing AI fails.

Script 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Manufacturing environments show us some of the most dramatic AI failure scenarios. Picture a sensor feeding slightly incorrect data to a robotic arm. That arm starts misplacing components, one after another, and before long you've got entire batches that have to be scrapped completely. Or consider an AI vision system that's supposed to spot defects. If it's miscalibrated, it might start rejecting perfectly good parts, slowing your throughput to a crawl. Now flip that around: a quality-check algorithm that's too lenient starts approving substandard goods, and those products make it all the way to customers, triggering expensive recalls. What's really striking is how these problems don't stay contained to one shift—they cascade through the entire production schedule, affecting multiple teams and creating costly delays that can take days to resolve.

Script 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Administrative failures might seem less dramatic than production problems, but they can be just as disruptive to daily operations. Your e-commerce system sends a batch of orders to completely wrong addresses overnight, with no human checking the output. Your inventory management tool quietly miscounts stock levels, so by morning your team is working from figures that don't match reality. Invoicing systems generate duplicate bills and send them straight to customers before anyone notices. Email routing fails, and important messages disappear into the void. Automated scheduling tools wipe entire calendars without warning. Each of these might sound extreme, but they're real failure modes that businesses encounter regularly. The common thread is that automation moved faster than any human could intervene, creating chaos in what should have been routine processes.

Script 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Here's what catches many businesses off guard: cascading failures, where one AI system breaks down and the damage spreads throughout the organization. Imagine an AI scheduling tool stops working correctly. Suddenly procurement gets delayed, which holds up production, which then pushes back shipments. Each department is waiting on the one before it. It gets worse when multiple teams are pulling from the same AI-generated data source. If that feed is corrupted, you're not just looking at one broken report—finance, operations, and customer service could all be working from bad information simultaneously, without any of them realizing it. When systems aren't properly isolated from one another, a single fault ripples outward and becomes everybody's problem very quickly. Understanding this domino effect is crucial for building more resilient workflows.

Script 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
When an AI failure hits, your priority is getting back on track as quickly and calmly as possible. First, activate whatever backup process you have in place so work doesn't grind to a halt. Simultaneously, notify the relevant teams about what's happening—nobody likes being caught off guard during a crisis. While things are being sorted, make sure someone documents exactly what went wrong, including timing and system behavior. That information will be invaluable for preventing future occurrences. In the meantime, your team should switch to manual workarounds to keep operations moving. Here's something people often skip: before you switch the AI back on, run some tests first. Don't assume it's fixed and dive straight back in. A quick verification now can save you from triggering the exact same problem all over again.

Script 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
The real value comes from treating every AI failure as useful information for improvement. When something goes wrong, document it, even if it seems minor at the time. Then dig into why it happened, not just what happened. Was it a poorly written prompt? A gap in the training data? A process that wasn't designed with AI limitations in mind? Once you understand the root cause, you can update your workflows so the same issue doesn't crop up again. Crucially, share what you learn with the rest of your team—if one person has already made a mistake, there's no reason for everyone else to repeat it. Create feedback loops that make it easy for staff to flag AI errors as they happen, so you can catch patterns early and address them before they become major disruptions.

Script 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Let's bring everything together with the core principles for reliable AI deployment. Most AI failures are predictable and preventable with the right safeguards in place. Always verify AI outputs before acting on them—even well-built systems can produce unexpected results. Many errors trace back to poor data quality at the source, so that's always worth examining first. Human oversight isn't optional; it's essential for catching problems before they escalate. Make sure your team knows exactly what to do when something looks wrong, because clear escalation procedures remove confusion during critical moments. Finally, resist the urge to deploy AI everywhere at once. Start with small, controlled implementations, test them thoroughly until you're confident in their reliability, and then expand gradually. These principles will help you harness AI's benefits while minimizing the risks we've discussed throughout this course.
