Title: What Is the Copy-Paste Risk?

Subtitle: When AI output becomes your liability

Content:
• AI-generated text copied directly into work without review
• Hidden risks in tone, accuracy, and legal ownership
• Looks polished but may contain serious problems
• A false sense of quality
• Affects documents, emails, code, and reports alike
• Your name on it means your responsibility

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Why Copy-Paste Risk Is So Common

Subtitle: The efficiency trap that catches everyone

Content:
• AI output appears professional and well-structured
• Feels like a genuine time-saver
• Easy to assume polished text is accurate and safe
• Skips the critical review step entirely
• Creates false confidence in the quality
• Habit spreads quickly across teams and organisations

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Warning Signs in AI Content

Subtitle: Red flags that demand your attention

Content:
• Vague or unverifiable claims
• Outdated statistics or dates
• Confident but incorrect facts
• Missing source references
• Generic, templated phrasing
• Unusual legal or technical terms

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Legal and Security Risks

Subtitle: The hidden dangers in AI outputs

Content:
• Copyright infringement from plagiarised text
• Code snippets with security vulnerabilities
• Privacy violations from exposed personal data
• Trademark issues from unattributed branded content
• Compliance breaches in regulated industries
• Liability for fabricated or defamatory statements

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Types of Fabricated Content

Subtitle: When AI makes things up convincingly

Content:
• Statistics and research findings that don't exist
• Fake citations and non-existent sources
• Made-up case studies and examples
• Invented quotes from real people
• Fictional company names and contact details
• Outdated information presented as current

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Pre-Copy Review Checklist

Subtitle: Essential checks before you paste

Content:
• Verify all facts, figures, and statistics independently
• Check for unusual phrasing that signals copied text
• Scan for personal or sensitive data exposure
• Review any code for security vulnerabilities
• Confirm all sources and citations actually exist
• Ask: Would I defend every word as my own work?

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Building Safe AI Habits

Subtitle: Practical steps for responsible use

Content:
• Always edit AI output in your own voice
• Run suspicious text through plagiarism checkers
• Never input confidential data into AI tools
• Verify sources independently before citing
• Treat AI output as rough drafts, not final copy
• Document your review process for important work

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Your Ongoing Responsibility

Subtitle: What to remember every time

Content:
• You remain accountable for everything you publish
• AI is a tool, not a replacement for professional judgment
• Quality control cannot be automated away
• Legal liability follows the person, not the AI
• Your reputation depends on consistent verification
• Professional standards still apply to AI-assisted work
