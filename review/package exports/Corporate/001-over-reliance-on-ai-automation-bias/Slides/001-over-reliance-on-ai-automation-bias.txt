Title: What Is Automation Bias?

Subtitle: Trusting machines more than we should

Content:
• The tendency to over-trust automated systems and accept their outputs without question
• A well-documented cognitive bias
• Occurs when people reduce their own critical thinking because a machine has already "decided"
• Common in high risk fields like medicine, aviation, and finance
• Not a new problem, but growing fast as AI becomes part of everyday work
• The machine feels reliable, so we stop double-checking

Title: When AI Gets It Wrong

Subtitle: Confident errors we fail to catch

Content:
• AI sounds certain, isn't
• Wrong answers, confident tone
• Errors slip past unnoticed
• We stop double-checking
• Mistakes reach real decisions

Title: Spotting Automation Bias in Practice

Subtitle: Real examples, familiar patterns revealed

Content:
• A sat-nav confidently routing drivers into a flooded road, and drivers following it anyway
• Doctors accepting AI diagnostic suggestions without reviewing the patient's actual symptoms
• Spell-checkers approved without reading
• Recruitment tools flagging candidates as unsuitable, with hiring managers never questioning the score
• Pilots disengaging from manual oversight because the autopilot "has it covered"

Title: Rebuilding Trust in Your Judgement

Subtitle: Reclaim confidence in your own thinking.

Content:
• Start small by making your own decisions without consulting AI first
• Notice when your instincts turn out to be right
• Keep a brief log of times your judgement caught something AI missed
• Practise explaining your reasoning aloud before checking any AI output
• Trust builds gradually, not all at once
