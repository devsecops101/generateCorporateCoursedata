Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Assumptions Hidden in the Code

Subtitle: What the algorithm never tells you

Content:
• Training data drawn from past projects, embedding outdated load standards silently
• No warning when regional codes differ
• Optimised for efficiency, not safety margins
• Material properties assumed uniform unless explicitly told otherwise
• Boundary conditions (fixed, pinned, free) defaulted without flagging the choice to the engineer
• Cost and weight minimisation baked in as invisible objectives

Slide 5 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Load Path Assumptions Gone Wrong

Subtitle: Where forces travel matters enormously.

Content:
• AI tools sometimes assume forces travel through the most obvious path, not the actual structural path
• A misread load path can shift stress into members never designed to carry it
• Connections fail first
• Seemingly minor geometry changes, like an offset beam, can reroute forces entirely
• AI may not flag when a proposed layout contradicts the intended load distribution

Title: Validating AI Design Outputs

Subtitle: Checking outputs before they become problems

Content:
• Check loads manually first
• Verify material specs always
• Cross-reference building codes
• Question unusual geometry outputs
• Confirm connection details independently

Slide 7 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Mitigating Hidden Assumption Risks

Subtitle: Strategies to Reduce Unseen Design Errors

Content:
• Always verify AI-generated outputs against established structural codes and standards
• Cross-check load assumptions manually
• Involve a qualified structural engineer at every review stage, not just at final sign-off
• Document every assumption the AI tool makes, so nothing stays hidden
• Run sensitivity checks on key variables like material strength and load distribution
• Build in a dedicated review step for site-specific conditions the AI may not have accounted for
