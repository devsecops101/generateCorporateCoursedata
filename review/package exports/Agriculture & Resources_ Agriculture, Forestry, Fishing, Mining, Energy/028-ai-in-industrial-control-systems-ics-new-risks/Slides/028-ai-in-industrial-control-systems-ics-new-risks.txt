Slide 1 (Slide 1)

Title: AI and ICS: An Introduction

Subtitle: Where operational technology meets artificial intelligence

Content:
• Industrial control systems (ICS) manage physical processes like power grids, water treatment, and manufacturing lines
• AI is increasingly embedded in these environments to optimise performance and predict faults
• New capability, new risk
• Every AI component added to an ICS creates a potential entry point for attackers
• Understanding these risks is the first step toward protecting critical infrastructure

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Legacy Systems Meet Modern Threats

Subtitle: Why traditional defences fall short against AI-powered attacks

Content:
• ICS were designed decades before AI existed, leaving security gaps that modern threats exploit
• AI enables dynamic, adaptive attacks that evade signature-based detection
• Attackers use AI to learn normal system patterns and disguise malicious activity as routine operations
• Legacy and new systems running side by side create unpredictable vulnerabilities
• Traditional security tools struggle to identify AI-driven attack patterns

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: New Attack Vectors in AI-Enhanced ICS

Subtitle: How AI components expand the attack surface

Content:
• Machine learning models and data pipelines create entirely new entry points
• Training data corruption allows attackers to influence decisions before operators notice
• Remote connectivity for AI updates significantly widens exposure
• AI-powered anomaly detection tools can be manipulated to ignore genuine threats
• Each connected AI component multiplies potential vulnerabilities

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Critical AI Vulnerabilities in Industrial Settings

Subtitle: Where AI creates exploitable weak points

Content:
• Biased or corrupted training data leads to dangerous operational decisions
• Opaque AI models hide failures until catastrophic outcomes occur
• Sensor spoofing tricks AI systems with false environmental data
• Automated responses can trigger unpredictable cascading effects
• Black box algorithms make troubleshooting and forensics nearly impossible

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Supply Chain AI Security Risks

Subtitle: Hidden threats in third-party AI components

Content:
• Pre-trained models may contain hidden backdoors or data poisoning
• Third-party libraries introduce unverified code into critical systems
• Vendor updates can silently introduce new vulnerabilities
• Many AI suppliers lack rigorous security audit processes
• Compromised shared libraries can affect multiple industrial systems

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Operational Impact of AI Compromises

Subtitle: Real-world consequences of AI security failures

Content:
• Manufacturing disruption from manipulated production algorithms
• Power grid instability through compromised load balancing systems
• Water treatment contamination via altered chemical dosing controls
• Equipment damage from AI-driven maintenance schedule corruption
• Safety system failures when AI bypasses critical protection mechanisms

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Defending AI-Integrated ICS Networks

Subtitle: Practical strategies to protect AI-enhanced systems

Content:
• Segment AI components from core ICS networks using strict access controls
• Monitor AI model inputs and outputs for unusual patterns signaling manipulation
• Validate training data sources to prevent poisoned datasets
• Limit AI system privileges to prevent direct critical command execution
• Maintain regular patching schedules for all AI components and dependencies

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Building AI Security Awareness

Subtitle: Preparing your team for AI-related threats

Content:
• Train operations staff to recognise abnormal AI behaviour patterns
• Establish incident response procedures specific to AI security events
• Create documentation standards for AI system configurations and changes
• Develop testing protocols for AI component security before deployment
• Foster a security-first culture when adopting new AI technologies
