Title: Sensor Spoofing: An Introduction

Subtitle: When Data Lies, Decisions Fail

Content:
• Sensors collect real-world data that machines and operators rely on to make critical decisions
• When that data is falsified, systems respond to conditions that do not actually exist
• Spoofing means feeding false inputs
• Attackers can manipulate temperature, pressure, flow, or location readings without touching physical equipment
• The consequences range from minor inefficiencies to catastrophic equipment failure or safety incidents

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: How Sensor Spoofing Works

Subtitle: The mechanics of deception

Content:
• Attackers intercept or replace legitimate sensor communications
• False data is injected between sensors and control systems
• Systems trust incoming data without verifying its authenticity
• Automated responses trigger based on fabricated readings
• No physical access to equipment required—attacks happen through data streams

Title: Critical Infrastructure Attacks

Subtitle: When spoofing targets essential services

Content:

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Protective Measures and Response

Subtitle: Defending against sensor spoofing

Content:
• Verify readings from multiple independent sources
• Implement data validation checks and range limits
• Monitor for sudden or unexplained changes in sensor data
• Use encrypted communications between sensors and systems
• Report anomalies immediately to security teams
