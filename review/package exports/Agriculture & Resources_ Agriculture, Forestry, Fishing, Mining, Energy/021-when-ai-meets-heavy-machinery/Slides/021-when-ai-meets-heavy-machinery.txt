Title: Autonomous Equipment and Hidden Risks

Subtitle: Where automation conceals unexpected dangers

Content:
• Autonomous tractors and drilling rigs can operate without a human physically present, removing a key layer of real-time judgement
• Sensors and algorithms make split-second decisions that operators may not see coming
• Hidden risks emerge when machines behave unexpectedly in conditions they were not trained to handle
• Blind spots in AI programming
• A system that appears to be working correctly can still be moving toward a hazardous situation
• Workers nearby may assume the machine "knows" what it is doing, reducing their own alertness

Title: Sensor Failures and Blind Spots

Subtitle: When sensors fail, machines lose awareness.

Content:
• Sensors are the "eyes" of AI-controlled machinery, detecting obstacles, terrain, and position
• Dirt, dust, and vibration can block or damage sensors without any visible warning
• A blind spot
• When one sensor fails, the AI may act on incomplete or misleading data
• Autonomous equipment can make dangerous decisions based on what it cannot see
• Regular sensor checks are as critical as any mechanical maintenance

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Staying Safe With AI Equipment

Subtitle: Practical steps to protect yourself daily

Content:
• Always read the operator manual before working alongside any AI-assisted machine
• Check that sensors and cameras are clean and unobstructed at the start of each shift
• Stay visible
• Never assume the system has detected you, confirm it has
• Report unusual machine behaviour to your supervisor immediately, even if the job seems urgent
• Keep bystanders and colleagues well clear of autonomous equipment operating zones

Title: What to Remember

Subtitle: Essential points to carry with you

Content:
• AI in heavy machinery can act faster than any human can react, making pre-checks critical
• Always verify sensor data manually before trusting an automated decision
• Autonomous equipment still needs a trained human nearby
• Know your override controls before the machine moves
• Report every near-miss, even if the system "corrected itself"
• Start small, one automated feature at a time, until you understand how it behaves in your conditions
