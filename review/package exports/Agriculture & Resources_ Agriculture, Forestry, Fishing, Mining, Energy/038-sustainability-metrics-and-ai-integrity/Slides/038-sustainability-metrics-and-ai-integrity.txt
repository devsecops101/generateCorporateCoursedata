Title: The Data Quality Problem

Subtitle: Poor inputs corrupt AI outputs

Content:
• AI amplifies whatever data you feed it
• Incomplete emissions records skew carbon calculations
• Outdated supplier data corrupts scope 3 reporting
• Errors compound throughout the system
• Biased datasets produce systematically flawed results

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: AI Knowledge Limitations

Subtitle: What AI systems don't know

Content:
• Training data cuts off months before your reporting date
• No live updates on regulations or emissions factors
• New standards like TCFD or CSRD may be missing
• Regional and sector-specific data underrepresented
• AI won't tell you when information is outdated

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Spotting AI Data Gaps

Subtitle: Where AI knowledge falls short

Content:
• Check if regulatory updates are reflected
• Verify emissions factors against current sources
• Test sector-specific calculations for accuracy
• Look for placeholder data in complex scenarios
• Cross-reference against authoritative databases

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Auditing AI-Generated ESG Data

Subtitle: Verifying what the algorithms produce

Content:
• Trace figures back to original sources
• Compare AI outputs to raw source material
• Watch for patterns of repeated errors
• Flag vague or missing data points
• Require human review at every stage

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Verification Best Practices

Subtitle: Steps to ensure AI output integrity

Content:
• Cross-check key figures manually
• Question unexpected or outlier results
• Document all AI processes and outputs
• Maintain clear audit trails
• Review thoroughly before publication

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Implementation Strategy

Subtitle: How to use AI responsibly in ESG reporting

Content:
• Start with low-risk internal reports
• Build verification processes before high-stakes use
• Train teams on AI limitations and risks
• Establish clear documentation standards
• Never skip human oversight for regulatory submissions
