Title: Deepfakes in the Workplace

Subtitle: When Seeing Is No Longer Believing

Content:
• Realistic fake videos and audio clips are now easy to create and hard to spot
• Field workers and operators increasingly receive instructions via digital channels
• A convincing fake voice or face can trigger dangerous real-world actions
• The threat is real
• This course explains what deepfakes are, how they appear in operational settings, and what you can do about them

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Visual Deepfake Detection

Subtitle: What to Watch For in Video Instructions

Content:
• Lip sync looks slightly off or delayed
• Facial edges flicker or appear blurry
• Unnatural blinking patterns (too frequent, rare, or oddly timed)
• Lighting shifts inconsistently mid-sentence
• Background details blur or glitch unexpectedly

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Audio Deepfake Detection

Subtitle: What to Listen For in Voice Instructions

Content:
• Voice sounds flat, robotic, or unnaturally smooth
• Background noise cuts abruptly or sounds too clean
• Voice pitch feels "off" or inconsistent
• Words seem clipped or artificially stitched together
• Caller rushes you or creates unusual pressure

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Behavioral Red Flags

Subtitle: When Instructions Don't Feel Right

Content:
• Request feels out of character for that person
• Unusual urgency without clear justification
• Bypassing normal approval processes
• Instructions contradict established safety protocols
• Caller avoids answering direct questions about details

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Real-World Scenarios

Subtitle: How Deepfakes Target Field Operations

Content:
• Fake supervisor voice messages about equipment changes
• Video calls impersonating managers during shift changes
• False emergency instructions from supposed safety officers
• Fraudulent client requests for operational modifications
• Counterfeit training videos with dangerous procedures

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Verification Strategies

Subtitle: Double-Check Before You Act

Content:
• Call the person back using a known, trusted number
• Verify through official company communication channels
• Check with a second supervisor or colleague
• Ask specific questions only the real person would know
• Follow standard authorization procedures regardless of urgency

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Reporting Suspicious Instructions

Subtitle: Speak Up, Stay Safe, Act Fast

Content:
• Report to your line manager or designated security contact immediately
• Use your organisation's official incident reporting channel
• Never delete the suspicious message before logging it
• Screenshot or record source details if safe to do so
• Speed matters - early reports help protect the whole team

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Points to Remember

Subtitle: Essential Habits for Staying Protected

Content:
• Always verify instructions through a second, trusted channel before acting
• Pause if something feels off - trust your instincts
• Unusual urgency is a common tactic to bypass judgment
• Check the source, not just the message content
• Report suspected deepfakes immediately, even if unsure
• Staying alert protects you and your whole team
