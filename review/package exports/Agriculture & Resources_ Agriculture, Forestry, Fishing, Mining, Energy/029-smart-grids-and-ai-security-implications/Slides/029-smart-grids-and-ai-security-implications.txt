Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: AI Training and Data Vulnerabilities

Subtitle: How historical bias and manipulation create risks

Content:
• AI systems trained on historical data may misread unusual but legitimate demand spikes
• Adversarial inputs (small, deliberate data tweaks) can silently steer AI decisions in dangerous directions
• Overconfident predictions with no uncertainty flagging
• Single points of failure in data sources can cascade

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: AI Operational Failures

Subtitle: When automated decisions go wrong

Content:
• Sensor malfunctions trigger cascading grid responses
• AI models cannot explain their reasoning to operators
• System interconnectedness amplifies small errors
• Diagnosis and correction become slower during incidents

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: AI Design Trade-offs and Limitations

Subtitle: How optimization choices create vulnerabilities

Content:
• AI optimized for efficiency may sacrifice resilience
• Systems appear stable under normal conditions but fail under stress
• Limited ability to handle unprecedented scenarios
• Performance metrics may not capture security considerations

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Foundational Security Measures

Subtitle: Basic protections for AI-managed grids

Content:
• Patch systems regularly and promptly
• Encrypt all grid data transmissions
• Train staff to recognize emerging threats
• Monitor networks continuously for anomalies
• Limit AI system access rights

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Advanced Resilience Strategies

Subtitle: Sophisticated approaches to grid protection

Content:
• Segment networks to contain attacks and errors
• Audit AI decision logs for unusual patterns
• Maintain human override capabilities at all times
• Deploy independent backup systems
• Share threat intelligence with security agencies

Title: Implementation Roadmap

Subtitle: Practical steps for secure smart grid deployment

Content:
• Plan incident response before crises occur
