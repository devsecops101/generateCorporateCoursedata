Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Early Warning Signs

Subtitle: Red flags that indicate AI system problems

Content:
• Missing or incomplete data in reports
• Figures that never change between periods
• No visible audit trail for decisions
• Automated alerts repeatedly ignored
• Unexplained gaps versus regulatory expectations

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Proactive Detection Methods

Subtitle: Catching problems before they reach regulators

Content:
• Regular spot-checks against source data
• Staff trained to recognize incorrect outputs
• Clear audit trails for AI-generated reports
• Documentation of when and how reports were created
• Systematic review of threshold compliance
• Tracking small errors before they compound

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Advanced Warning Indicators

Subtitle: Subtle signs of AI system dysfunction

Content:
• Reports never flag violations in high-risk operations
• Suspiciously uniform or round emissions figures
• Dashboards updating without human sign-off
• Discrepancies between summaries and raw data
• Staff unable to explain system conclusions
• Automatic updates bypassing review processes

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Essential Human Oversight

Subtitle: Keeping humans firmly in the loop

Content:
• Assign named reviewers for all AI outputs
• Question unusual data patterns before proceeding
• Require human approval before report submission
• Log every human decision and override
• Document review processes for audit purposes
• Maintain accountability at every stage

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Risk Management Framework

Subtitle: Systematic approach to AI compliance safety

Content:
• Regular audits of AI reports versus source data
• Clear escalation paths for inconsistent outputs
• Staff training on error identification
• Process documentation for regulatory defense
• Review cycles when regulations change
• Established verification workflows before submission

Title: Key Implementation Principles

Subtitle: Core practices for responsible AI compliance

Content:
• Never submit without human verification
• Maintain legal accountability awareness
• Build systematic checking habits
• Document all oversight activities
• Treat AI as assistant, not replacement
• Prepare for regulatory scrutiny
