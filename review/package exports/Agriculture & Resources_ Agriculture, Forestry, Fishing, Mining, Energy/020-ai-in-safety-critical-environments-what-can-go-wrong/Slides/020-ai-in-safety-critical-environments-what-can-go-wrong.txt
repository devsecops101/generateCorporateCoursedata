Title: Signals That Warn Us Early

Subtitle: Before harm becomes an incident

Content:
• Unexpected sensor silence
• Repeated small alerts ignored
• Outputs that "almost" match
• Staff overriding warnings often
• Logs showing frequent corrections

Slide 4 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Sensor Failures and False Readings

Subtitle: When sensors mislead, AI follows suit.

Content:
• Sensors measure temperature, pressure, gas levels, and movement in hazardous environments
• A faulty sensor sends bad data
• AI systems trained to trust sensor input will act on that bad data without question
• In a mine or on an oil rig, a false "all clear" reading can delay a critical safety response
• Sensor degradation from dust, heat, or moisture is common in these settings
• No built-in scepticism means AI cannot tell the difference between a real reading and a corrupted one

Slide 6 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Lessons From Real Failures

Subtitle: What history teaches us about risk

Content:
• Early automation failures in mining (like sensor misreads triggering false "all clear" signals) led to updated safety protocols still used today
• Aviation's near-misses shaped how we now think about human oversight of automated systems
• Lessons rarely come cheap
• The Deepwater Horizon disaster highlighted how automated warnings can be dismissed or overridden under pressure
• Repeated failures across industries show that complexity and speed are the two biggest enemies of safe AI deployment
• Small errors in high risk environments rarely stay small

Slide 7 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Lessons Learned, Moving Forward

Subtitle: Applying insights to prevent future failures

Content:
• Test AI before deployment
• Train staff regularly
• Review failures honestly
• Update systems often
• Share lessons across teams

Slide 8 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What to Remember

Subtitle: Core lessons to carry forward always.

Content:
• AI errors in farms, rigs, and mines can cause real physical harm, not just data problems
• Always verify AI outputs with human judgement before acting
• Context matters
• Faulty sensor data fed into AI systems can silently trigger dangerous decisions
• Report unexpected AI behaviour immediately, even if nothing goes wrong
• Start by identifying one AI-assisted process in your workplace and asking who checks its outputs
