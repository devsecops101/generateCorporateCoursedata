Title: Introducing Automation Bias

Subtitle: When Automation Shapes Pilot Decision-Making

Content:
• Modern cockpits rely heavily on automated systems for navigation, alerts, and control
• Pilots can begin to trust automation more than their own judgement
• This over-reliance is known as automation bias
• A small shift in trust can lead to serious decision-making errors
• Affects both experienced pilots and operators across aviation and beyond

Slide 3 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What Is Automation Bias?

Subtitle: Trusting Automation Over Human Judgement

Content:
• Trusting machines over yourself
• Ignoring warnings, following automation
• Reduces active human monitoring
• Common in cockpits and control rooms
• Can cause critical errors fast

Slide 4 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Recognising the Warning Signs

Subtitle: When Automation Quietly Erodes Your Vigilance

Content:
• Ignoring unexpected system alerts
• Skipping manual cross-checks
• Trusting displays without verifying
• Reduced situational awareness overall
• Delayed response to anomalies

Slide 7 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Reducing Automation Bias Going Forward

Subtitle: Strategies for Safer Human-Machine Interaction

Content:
• Regular manual flying practice to maintain core skills without automation support
• Treat system alerts as meaningful, not background noise
• Cross-check automated outputs against independent sources whenever possible
• Build habits of active monitoring rather than passive watching
• Question the system, especially in unfamiliar or degraded conditions
• Organisations should train crews to recognise the early signs of over-reliance

Slide 8 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Points to Remember

Subtitle: Essential reminders for safer automated operations

Content:
• Always verify automated outputs with your own situational awareness
• Trust your training, not just the screen
• Automation can fail silently, without any obvious warning
• Regularly practise manual skills to prevent over-reliance from setting in
• Question unexpected system behaviour rather than assuming the system is correct
• Review your organisation's guidance on automation use and raise concerns early
