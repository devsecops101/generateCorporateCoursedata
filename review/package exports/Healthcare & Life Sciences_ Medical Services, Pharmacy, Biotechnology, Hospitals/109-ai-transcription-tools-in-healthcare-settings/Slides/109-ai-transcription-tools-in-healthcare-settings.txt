Title: Using AI in Healthcare

Subtitle: Balancing Innovation With Patient Safety

Content:
• AI transcription tools now widely used in GP surgeries, hospitals, and clinics across the UK
• Saves clinicians time on documentation
• But these tools carry real risks around patient privacy and accuracy
• Understanding those risks is essential before adopting any tool
• This course gives practical, beginner-friendly guidance for safe use

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Overview of Transcription Risks

Subtitle: What can go wrong, and why

Content:
• Misheard words leading to incorrect medication names or dosages in patient notes
• Patient data sent to external servers without clear consent
• Accidental recording of sensitive disclosures
• Transcripts stored insecurely or shared beyond intended recipients
• Dialect and accent variation reducing accuracy for some patients
• No automatic flag when the tool is uncertain or has made an error

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Clinical Accuracy Challenges

Subtitle: Common transcription errors in healthcare

Content:
• Homophones create dangerous confusion (e.g., "hyper" vs "hypo")
• Medical jargon and terminology frequently misinterpreted
• Background noise in clinical environments affects accuracy
• Complex drug names sound similar and get confused
• Accents and speech patterns reduce reliability for some patients
• AI systems lack medical context to catch implausible results

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Error Propagation in Records

Subtitle: How mistakes spread through patient care

Content:
• Unchecked transcription errors persist in permanent medical records
• Downstream clinicians rely on inaccurate information without knowing the source
• Wrong patient details can attach to incorrect medical files
• Missed allergies or contraindications create safety risks
• Errors compound when notes are referenced for future treatment decisions
• Manual review is the only reliable way to catch mistakes before they spread

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Privacy and Data Security Risks

Subtitle: Protecting sensitive patient information

Content:
• Audio recordings may be transmitted to third-party servers without patient knowledge
• Sensitive disclosures captured inadvertently during consultations
• Data retention policies unclear with many transcription services
• Patient identifiers embedded in transcripts create privacy exposure
• Cloud storage without proper data processing agreements violates regulations
• No guarantee of secure deletion when switching tools or providers

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Minimising Risk in Practice

Subtitle: Practical steps to reduce everyday risks

Content:
• Always verify transcribed notes before saving or sharing
• Anonymise patient data immediately after transcription
• Use only organisation-approved tools with proper contracts
• Log and report transcription errors to improve systems
• Obtain explicit patient consent before recording consultations
• Delete audio recordings promptly after transcription is complete

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Choosing Appropriate Tools

Subtitle: Evaluating transcription solutions for clinical use

Content:
• Verify data processing agreements meet healthcare standards
• Ensure tools comply with GDPR and medical confidentiality requirements
• Assess accuracy performance with medical terminology and diverse accents
• Check for local data storage options vs cloud-based processing
• Confirm audit trails and error reporting capabilities
• Avoid consumer-grade apps that lack clinical safeguards

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Points to Remember

Subtitle: Core principles for safe, responsible use

Content:
• Always verify AI transcriptions before finalising notes
• Protect patient data through proper consent and anonymisation
• Report transcription errors to help improve safety systems
• Use only approved tools with appropriate data agreements
• Review all notes carefully before adding to medical records
• Remember that human oversight remains essential for patient safety
