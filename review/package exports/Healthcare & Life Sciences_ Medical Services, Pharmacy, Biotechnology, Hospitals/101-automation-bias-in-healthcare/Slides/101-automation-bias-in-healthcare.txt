Title: Automation Bias in Healthcare

Subtitle: When AI Confidence Overrides Clinical Judgement

Content:
• AI tools are increasingly used to support clinical decisions in healthcare settings
• Clinicians sometimes accept AI outputs without applying their own judgement
• This tendency is known as automation bias
• It can lead to missed diagnoses, delayed treatment, or patient harm
• Understanding the risk is the first step toward safer AI-assisted practice

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Defining Automation Bias

Subtitle: When AI judgement replaces our own

Content:
• Trusting AI over your own judgement
• Skipping checks when AI agrees
• Common in high-pressure environments
• Subtle, often goes unnoticed
• Affects even experienced clinicians

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Recognising Automation Bias in Practice

Subtitle: Spotting the signs before harm occurs

Content:
• Accepting an AI-generated diagnosis without checking it against your own clinical findings
• Skipping a second look
• Feeling reluctant to override an alert because "the system flagged it"
• Failing to document your own reasoning when an AI recommendation is already on screen
• Noticing that team discussions shorten when an AI output is visible in the room

Slide 5 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Recognising Bias in Your Practice

Subtitle: Spotting the signs in everyday decisions

Content:
• Accepting alerts without questioning
• Skipping your own assessment
• Feeling reassured too quickly
• Deferring when unsure of yourself
• Ignoring contradicting patient signs

Slide 7 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Reducing Automation Bias in Practice

Subtitle: Practical steps to stay critically engaged

Content:
• Pause before accepting AI output
• Question unexpected normal results
• Involve colleagues in decisions
• Trust your clinical instincts
• Document your own reasoning

Slide 8 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Points to Remember

Subtitle: What every clinician should bear in mind

Content:
• AI tools can suggest, but clinicians must always verify
• Automation bias grows stronger under time pressure and fatigue
• A confident-looking output is not the same as a correct one
• Build in deliberate pauses to question AI recommendations before acting
• Your clinical judgement remains the final safeguard
• Start small: identify one workflow where you can practise active oversight this week
