Title: Deepfake Risks in Healthcare

Subtitle: When Deception Threatens Patient Safety

Content:
• Deepfakes use artificial intelligence to create convincing fake audio, video, or images of real people
• A growing threat to healthcare
• Criminals can impersonate doctors, nurses, or administrators to manipulate patients and staff
• Fake medical instructions or fraudulent prescriptions can put lives at risk
• Healthcare workers and patients are increasingly targeted
• Understanding the risks is the first step to staying protected

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: How Deepfakes Target Healthcare

Subtitle: Common Attack Scenarios

Content:
• Video messages appearing to show doctors issuing revised medication instructions
• Phone calls impersonating consultants or senior clinicians
• Fake audio messages from pharmacy staff about prescription changes
• Fraudulent patient communications requesting treatment modifications
• False administrative directives affecting care protocols
• Attacks exploit trust relationships within healthcare teams

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Visual Warning Signs

Subtitle: Spotting Video Deepfakes

Content:
• Blurry or unnatural facial movement
• Lips not syncing perfectly with spoken words
• Odd lighting on the face compared to background
• Unnatural blinking patterns or eye movement
• Background distortions or inconsistencies
• Watch for these technical imperfections during video communications

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Audio Warning Signs

Subtitle: Detecting Voice Deepfakes

Content:
• Voice sounds slightly robotic or flat
• Unnatural pauses or rhythm in speech
• Audio quality inconsistencies
• Missing personal conversational details the real person would know
• Caller avoids direct questions or personal references
• Background noise that doesn't match the supposed location

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Content Red Flags

Subtitle: Suspicious Communication Patterns

Content:
• Unexpected changes to medication dosages or treatment plans
• Instructions arriving outside normal clinical channels
• Requests feel unusually urgent or bypass standard procedures
• Instructions that contradict established protocols
• Requests to share patient data through unofficial channels
• Messages that cannot be traced to documented sources

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Verification Strategies

Subtitle: Confirming Authenticity Before Acting

Content:
• Always verify through a separate, trusted communication channel
• Use official contact numbers from your organization's records
• Confirm identity with personal details only the real person would know
• Cross-check instructions against documented protocols
• Never act immediately on urgent requests without verification
• When in doubt, hang up and call back using official numbers

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Reporting Suspicious Activity

Subtitle: Steps to Report and Escalate Concerns

Content:
• Stop immediately - do not act on the suspicious instruction
• Notify your line manager without delay
• Document exactly what you observed while details are fresh
• Contact your IT security team for investigation
• Follow your organization's specific reporting procedures

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Essential Takeaways

Subtitle: Building Protective Habits

Content:
• Verify every clinical instruction through official channels before acting
• Trust your instincts when something feels unusual
• Remember that deepfakes can convincingly mimic real clinicians
• Urgency is often used to prevent careful verification
• Report suspicious content immediately to protect others
• Make verification a standard part of your professional routine
