Title: Using AI with EHRs

Subtitle: Risks, Rewards, and Responsible Use

Content:
• AI tools are increasingly being used alongside Electronic Health Records in clinical settings
• Understanding the risks matters
• Incorrect summarisation and data exposure are two of the most pressing concerns for healthcare staff
• This lesson covers both the potential benefits and the responsibilities that come with using AI in patient care
• No prior technical knowledge needed

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Data Exposure Risks

Subtitle: How Patient Information Can Be Compromised

Content:
• Sensitive data may be exposed if AI tools are not properly secured
• Consumer AI applications lack clinical-grade privacy protections
• Data can be stored on external servers without your knowledge
• Third-party AI vendors may have different data retention policies
• Audit trails can become unclear when AI contributes to clinical documentation

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Human Factors in AI Risk

Subtitle: Why Good Clinicians Make AI Mistakes

Content:
• Over-reliance develops when AI outputs look polished and confident
• Time pressure leads to skipping verification steps
• Trust in technology can reduce clinical scrutiny
• Errors can go unnoticed without proper oversight
• Multiple AI interactions create cumulative risk exposure

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Risks of Incorrect Summarisation

Subtitle: When AI Gets the Summary Wrong

Content:
• Wrong drugs listed
• Missed allergies
• Incorrect diagnoses recorded
• Outdated information shown
• Patient safety at risk

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Approved Tools and Data Protection

Subtitle: Safe AI Usage Guidelines

Content:
• Always use NHS-approved or trust-approved AI tools only
• Never paste identifiable patient data into consumer AI applications
• Understand your organisation's AI policy and approved vendor list
• Report any suspected data breach immediately using your organisation's standard incident process
• Keep a brief record of when and how AI was used in any clinical task

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Clinical Verification Protocols

Subtitle: Essential Checks for AI Outputs

Content:
• Double-check every AI-generated summary before acting on it
• Verify patient identity matches at multiple data points
• Cross-reference critical information with original records
• Look for temporal inconsistencies in patient history
• Document your verification process in clinical notes

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Protecting Patients From AI Errors

Subtitle: Safeguards every clinician should implement

Content:
• Always verify AI summaries
• Check patient details carefully
• Flag unexpected AI outputs
• Never skip manual review
• Report errors promptly

Title: Key Points to Remember

Subtitle: Protect data, verify AI outputs always.
