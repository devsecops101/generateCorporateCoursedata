Title: Introducing AI Triage Risks

Subtitle: When AI gets triage dangerously wrong

Content:
• AI triage systems sort patients by urgency, deciding who gets care first
• Sometimes the algorithm gets it wrong
• Urgent cases can be deprioritised, delayed, or missed entirely
• Real patients face real harm when this happens
• Understanding these risks is the first step to preventing them

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What Is Misclassification?

Subtitle: When AI assigns the wrong priority level

Content:
• AI incorrectly ranks patient urgency
• Serious cases marked as low priority
• Non-urgent cases flagged as critical
• Errors appear legitimate on the surface
• Staff may not immediately question AI decisions

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Technical Causes of Misclassification

Subtitle: System limitations that lead to errors

Content:
• Training data underrepresents certain patient groups
• Poor input quality causes misreading of symptoms
• Rare conditions absent from training datasets
• Systems overweight single metrics while ignoring context
• Informal symptom descriptions not recognised
• Historical bias embedded in learning algorithms

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Common Vulnerability Patterns

Subtitle: Which patients are most at risk

Content:
• Older adults and non-native speakers
• Patients with atypical symptom presentations
• Those with rare but serious conditions
• Cases requiring complex contextual assessment
• Patients describing symptoms in non-medical language

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Impact of Delayed Critical Care

Subtitle: How misclassification harms patients

Content:
• Time-sensitive conditions like stroke and sepsis deteriorate rapidly
• Wrong priority queue means extended waiting times
• Permanent damage can occur within minutes
• Families and clinicians lack accurate information for decisions
• Patient outcomes worsen significantly with delay

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Beyond Patient Harm

Subtitle: Wider consequences of triage errors

Content:
• Healthcare system loses patient and family trust
• Legal liability for preventable outcomes
• Staff morale affected by missed cases
• Resource allocation becomes inefficient
• Quality metrics and reputation suffer

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Prevention Strategies

Subtitle: Building safer AI triage processes

Content:
• Regular audits comparing AI outputs to patient outcomes
• Mandatory clinician override capabilities
• Frequent training data updates reflecting current demographics
• Clear escalation protocols for low-confidence AI decisions
• Staff education on critical evaluation of AI recommendations
• Error feedback loops for continuous system improvement

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Essential Takeaways

Subtitle: Your role in safer AI triage

Content:
• Never assume AI has identified all urgent cases
• Apply independent clinical judgement to verify outputs
• Maintain human oversight as essential safety layer
• Report suspected errors to improve system performance
• Advocate for diverse, high-quality training data
• Stay alert to bias patterns in AI recommendations
