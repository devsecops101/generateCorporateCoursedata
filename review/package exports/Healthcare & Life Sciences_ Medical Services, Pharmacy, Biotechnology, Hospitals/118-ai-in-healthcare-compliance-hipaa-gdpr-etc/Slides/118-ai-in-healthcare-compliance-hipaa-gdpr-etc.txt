Title: Why AI Raises Compliance Risks

Subtitle: Unpacking the hidden risks of AI

Content:
• AI processes sensitive patient data
• Errors can harm real people
• Bias hides in training data
• Audits become harder to conduct
• Regulations struggle to keep pace

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: HIPAA and GDPR Exposure Points

Subtitle: Where well-intentioned AI deployments breach regulations

Content:
• Automated clinical decisions trigger additional legal obligations
• Incomplete audit trails prevent accountability demonstration
• Regulators scrutinise both AI vendors and healthcare users
• Non-compliance results in fines, reputational damage, and lost patient trust
• Even careful implementations can breach regulations without proper safeguards

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Data Minimisation Principles

Subtitle: Why collecting only essential data reduces risk

Content:
• GDPR and HIPAA require collecting only strictly necessary data
• Excess patient data creates larger regulatory targets
• More data means more breach and misuse potential
• Anonymising data before AI processing reduces compliance exposure
• Regulators question whether AI data consumption justifies volume

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Implementing Data Limits

Subtitle: Practical design choices to minimise compliance burden

Content:
• Restrict AI access to specific data fields only
• Address over-collection at the design stage
• Smaller data inputs create smaller breach surfaces
• Fewer inputs simplify audits and reduce penalties
• Design decisions directly impact compliance liability

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Ongoing Data Governance

Subtitle: Regular monitoring prevents compliance drift

Content:
• Audit AI data inputs regularly
• Data requirements can expand without notice
• Strip non-essential information before training
• Monitor for gradual increases in data collection
• Maintain strict purpose limitation throughout AI lifecycle

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Audit Trails and Accountability

Subtitle: Tracking every AI decision made

Content:
• AI logs every action automatically
• Records who approved each decision
• Timestamps prove regulatory compliance
• Auditors can trace AI choices
• Gaps in logs mean liability

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Reducing Your Compliance Risk

Subtitle: Practical steps to protect your organisation

Content:
• Audit all AI tools used
• Train staff on data rules
• Document every AI decision
• Limit data access strictly
• Review vendor contracts regularly
• Report breaches without delay

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Points to Remember

Subtitle: Core principles for responsible AI use

Content:
• AI does not reduce your compliance obligations, it adds new ones
• Always know where patient data goes when it enters an AI tool
• Audit trails matter
• Consent, transparency, and data minimisation remain non-negotiable under GDPR and HIPAA
• Your organisation is accountable for AI outputs, even when the vendor is at fault
• As a next step, review your current AI tools against your data processing agreements and flag any gaps to your compliance team
