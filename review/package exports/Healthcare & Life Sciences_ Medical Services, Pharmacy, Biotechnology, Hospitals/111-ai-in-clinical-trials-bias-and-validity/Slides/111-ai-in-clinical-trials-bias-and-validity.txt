Title: AI in Clinical Trials: Overview

Subtitle: Understanding the Promise and Perils

Content:
• AI transforms clinical research workflows
• Accelerates patient identification and data analysis
• Introduces new types of bias risks
• Requires careful oversight and validation
• Human judgment remains essential

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Types of Bias in AI Systems

Subtitle: Where Problems Originate

Content:
• Historical bias: Learning from past inequities
• Selection bias: Skewed training datasets
• Algorithmic bias: Flawed model assumptions
• Confirmation bias: Favouring expected results
• Measurement bias: Inconsistent data collection

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Patient Selection Bias

Subtitle: How AI Perpetuates Recruitment Inequities

Content:
• AI trained on historical data reflects past exclusions
• Underrepresented groups filtered out automatically
• Geographic and socioeconomic biases in hospital networks
• Eligibility algorithms embed existing clinical preferences
• Results may not generalise to broader populations

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Real-World Selection Examples

Subtitle: Concrete Cases of AI Recruitment Bias

Content:
• Cardiovascular trials excluding women due to historical patterns
• Cancer research skewing toward younger patients
• Rare disease studies missing ethnic diversity
• Mental health trials favouring urban populations
• Automated screening missing comorbid conditions

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Analysis and Interpretation Bias

Subtitle: When AI Distorts Data Processing

Content:
• Pattern recognition prioritises speed over accuracy
• Confounding variables go undetected
• Statistical assumptions built into algorithms
• Outcome definitions influenced by training data
• Small systematic errors compound at scale

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Strategies to Reduce Bias

Subtitle: Practical Steps for Fairer Trials

Content:
• Pre-register analysis plans before data collection
• Use diverse, representative training datasets
• Implement regular algorithmic audits
• Apply standardised reporting frameworks (CONSORT-AI)
• Maintain detailed audit trails of AI decisions

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Reporting and Publication Transparency

Subtitle: Preventing Selective Disclosure

Content:
• Disclose all AI tools and training data sources
• Report negative and null results equally
• Include independent reviewer validation
• Document known limitations and uncertainties
• Share methodology for reproducibility

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Building Responsible AI Practices

Subtitle: Your Next Steps Forward

Content:
• Audit current datasets for representation gaps
• Establish bias monitoring protocols
• Train teams on AI limitation awareness
• Collaborate with diverse stakeholders
• Question every automated recommendation
