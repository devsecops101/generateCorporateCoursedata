Title: Ransomware and AI in Healthcare

Subtitle: When AI Meets Ransomware Risk

Content:
• Healthcare now relies on AI tools for diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient monitoring
• Ransomware targets these systems with growing frequency
• When AI-driven systems go down, the impact spreads fast
• Patient care suffers immediately
• This course explains the risks and how to respond

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What Makes AI Systems Attractive Targets

Subtitle: Why attackers focus on healthcare AI

Content:
• AI systems hold vast amounts of patient data
• Healthcare organisations often pay ransoms quickly due to life-or-death urgency
• Many AI platforms connect to multiple hospital systems
• Security gaps exist where legacy systems meet new AI tools
• High-value targets with significant disruption potential

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Immediate Impact on Clinical Care

Subtitle: When AI tools fail during active patient care

Content:
• Diagnostic imaging systems freeze mid-scan
• Drug interaction alerts disappear without warning
• Patient monitoring alarms stop functioning
• Treatment recommendations become unavailable
• Emergency protocols revert to manual processes

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Cascading Effects Across Hospital Operations

Subtitle: How AI dependency amplifies ransomware damage

Content:
• One compromised system affects interconnected workflows
• Staff lose decision-support tools simultaneously
• Manual backup procedures may be outdated or untested
• Recovery time extends as AI systems require specialized restoration
• Patient throughput slows significantly

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Time-Critical Scenarios

Subtitle: When seconds matter and AI fails

Content:
• Stroke diagnosis tools become unavailable during suspected cases
• Cardiac monitoring AI stops alerting to dangerous rhythms
• ICU systems lose predictive analytics for patient deterioration
• Emergency department triage algorithms go offline
• Operating room navigation systems halt mid-procedure

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Staff Adaptation Challenges

Subtitle: Human factors in AI-dependent environments

Content:
• Clinical staff trained primarily on AI-assisted workflows struggle with manual procedures
• Muscle memory relies on automated prompts and alerts
• Decision-making confidence decreases without AI support
• Workflow efficiency drops dramatically during system outages
• Training gaps emerge between experienced and newer staff

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Recovery Complications

Subtitle: Why AI systems take longer to restore

Content:
• AI models require extensive data validation before restart
• Machine learning algorithms need retraining after data corruption
• Interconnected systems must be restored in specific sequences
• Safety checks increase restoration time significantly
• Backup AI systems may also be compromised

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Building Resilience

Subtitle: Practical steps for protection and preparedness

Content:
• Maintain and test manual backup procedures regularly
• Implement network segmentation to isolate AI systems
• Train staff on both AI-assisted and traditional workflows
• Develop incident response plans specific to AI system failures
• Conduct regular security assessments of AI infrastructure
• Establish clear communication protocols during outages
