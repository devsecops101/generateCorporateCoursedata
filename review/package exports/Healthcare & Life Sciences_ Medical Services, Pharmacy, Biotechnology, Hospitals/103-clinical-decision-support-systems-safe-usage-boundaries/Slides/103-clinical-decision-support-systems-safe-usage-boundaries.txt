Title: Welcome and Course Overview

Subtitle: Defining where AI support must end

Content:
• Clinical decision support systems (CDSS) are becoming standard tools across NHS and private healthcare settings
• This course focuses on one critical question: where should AI assistance stop
• Designed for clinicians, nurses, and allied health professionals new to working alongside these systems
• No prior technical knowledge required
• You will leave with clear, practical boundaries you can apply in your daily practice
• Real-world scenarios throughout to ground every concept in clinical reality

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Fundamental AI Limitations in Healthcare

Subtitle: What AI cannot safely decide alone

Content:
• AI tools trained on population data, not your individual patient's full context
• Unable to account for a patient's values, fears, or personal circumstances
• No legal or professional accountability
• Cannot interpret ambiguous symptoms the way a clinician with physical presence can
• Blind spots in training data mean rare or atypical presentations are often missed

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Data Quality and Bias Issues

Subtitle: When historical data fails current patients

Content:
• Training data gaps and biases get embedded in AI recommendations
• Systems struggle with underrepresented populations and conditions
• Missing or incomplete patient records lead to flawed suggestions
• AI cannot detect when key information is absent from the dataset
• Population patterns may not apply to your specific patient demographics

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Real-Time Clinical Context

Subtitle: What happens between the data points

Content:
• AI cannot observe patient distress, sudden deterioration, or behavioral changes
• No awareness of family dynamics, social circumstances, or patient fears
• Cannot factor in clinical intuition from years of bedside experience
• Misses subtle cues that indicate something beyond the recorded symptoms
• Local protocols and institutional knowledge remain outside AI scope

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Complex and Rare Presentations

Subtitle: When patterns break down

Content:
• Rare conditions have insufficient training examples for reliable AI guidance
• Atypical symptom combinations confuse pattern-based algorithms
• Multi-comorbidity cases exceed most AI training scenarios
• Emergency situations require immediate human judgment, not delayed AI processing
• Novel conditions or drug interactions may not exist in training data

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Professional Accountability Boundaries

Subtitle: Where responsibility cannot be delegated

Content:
• AI carries no legal liability—you remain fully accountable for patient outcomes
• Professional bodies expect human oversight of all clinical decisions
• Insurance and medico-legal protection requires documented clinical reasoning
• Patient consent involves human judgment about risks and benefits
• Duty of care cannot be transferred to an algorithm

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: When to Override AI Recommendations

Subtitle: Trusting clinical instinct over algorithms

Content:
• Clinical gut feeling conflicts with AI suggestion—investigate further
• Patient presentation doesn't match algorithmic expectations
• High-stakes decisions require human confirmation regardless of AI confidence
• Vulnerable populations need individualized assessment beyond AI recommendations
• Emergency situations where human speed and adaptability are essential

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Safe Practice Framework

Subtitle: Implementing AI boundaries in daily practice

Content:
• Always verify AI suggestions against your clinical assessment
• Document your reasoning process, not just AI outputs
• Maintain heightened oversight for high-risk patients and situations
• Know your organization's CDSS policies and escalation procedures
• Regular training on system limitations and appropriate use cases
• Establish clear protocols for when to seek senior clinical input
