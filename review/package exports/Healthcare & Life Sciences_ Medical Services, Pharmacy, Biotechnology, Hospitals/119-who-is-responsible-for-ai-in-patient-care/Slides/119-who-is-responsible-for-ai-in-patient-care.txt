Title: The AI Accountability Challenge

Subtitle: Why clarity matters in healthcare AI

Content:
• AI tools are rapidly expanding across healthcare
• Multiple stakeholders share responsibility for outcomes
• Unclear boundaries create patient safety risks
• Accountability gaps must be identified and closed
• Clear frameworks protect everyone involved

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Stakeholder Ecosystem

Subtitle: Who shapes AI's role in patient care

Content:
• Technology vendors design and validate AI systems
• Healthcare organizations procure and deploy tools
• Clinicians interpret outputs for patient decisions
• Regulators establish safety and approval standards
• Patients experience the direct impact of AI choices
• Each group influences different aspects of AI implementation

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Clinician Responsibilities

Subtitle: Where AI meets bedside decision-making

Content:
• Clinicians make final decisions using AI as input
• Professional judgment cannot be delegated to algorithms
• Understanding tool limitations is a clinical duty
• Inadequate training creates dangerous knowledge gaps
• Documentation of AI-assisted decisions is essential

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Vendor Accountability

Subtitle: Building safe and reliable AI systems

Content:
• Vendors must ensure systems are clinically validated
• Transparent documentation of capabilities and limits
• Ongoing monitoring of real-world performance
• Clear labeling of intended use cases
• Responsibility extends beyond initial deployment

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Healthcare Organization Duties

Subtitle: Governance and implementation oversight

Content:
• Organizations establish AI governance policies
• Staff training and competency requirements
• Monitoring outcomes and system performance
• Risk assessment before tool deployment
• Creating accountability frameworks across teams

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: When Things Go Wrong

Subtitle: Handling AI-related adverse events

Content:
• Root cause analysis examines all contributing factors
• Technology failure vs. user error vs. system design
• Legal and regulatory reporting requirements
• Learning from incidents to prevent future harm
• Shared accountability doesn't mean shared blame

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Best Practices for Shared Accountability

Subtitle: Making distributed responsibility work

Content:
• Written agreements define specific role boundaries
• Regular communication between all stakeholders
• Continuous education on evolving AI capabilities
• Standardized incident reporting and analysis
• Culture of transparency and continuous improvement

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Your Role Going Forward

Subtitle: Practical steps for responsible AI use

Content:
• Never accept AI outputs without clinical judgment
• Document when and how AI influenced decisions
• Stay current on tools' capabilities and limitations
• Report concerns about AI performance promptly
• Maintain patient-centered focus in all AI interactions
