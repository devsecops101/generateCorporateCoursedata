Title: What Is False Confidence?

Subtitle: When AI certainty doesn't match reality

Content:
• AI displays confidence scores even when findings are wrong
• False confidence occurs when clinicians trust AI without critical review
• Missed tumors flagged as normal create direct patient safety risks
• Both over-detection and under-detection mislead clinical decisions
• Confidence percentages don't reflect real-world diagnostic accuracy

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: How Overreliance Develops

Subtitle: The gradual erosion of clinical skepticism

Content:
• High confidence scores bypass independent image review
• Anchoring bias makes clinicians defer to AI flags
• Overreliance grows gradually and often goes unnoticed
• Time pressure increases tendency to trust AI outputs
• Subtle findings get overlooked when AI appears certain

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Training Data Limitations

Subtitle: Why AI knowledge has blind spots

Content:
• Rare conditions underrepresented in training datasets
• Poor image quality confuses AI algorithms
• Certain patient populations may be excluded from training
• Unusual presentations not seen during AI development
• Dataset gaps create systematic weaknesses in AI performance

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Confidence Scores Explained

Subtitle: What those percentages really mean

Content:
• 95% confidence reflects model certainty, not diagnostic accuracy
• Scores based on training data behavior, not clinical outcomes
• High confidence can occur even with incorrect findings
• Score inflation happens with unrepresentative training datasets
• Early-stage lesions often misclassified with false certainty

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Common AI Failure Points

Subtitle: Where algorithms struggle most

Content:
• Rare conditions often completely missed
• Motion artifacts and poor image quality reduce accuracy
• Atypical disease presentations fool pattern recognition
• Edge cases outside training parameters
• Overlapping or complex pathologies challenge AI interpretation

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Reducing Risk in Practice

Subtitle: Practical steps to safeguard patient outcomes

Content:
• Always verify AI findings with independent clinical assessment
• Check both flagged and unflagged areas equally
• Use structured checklists to maintain diagnostic rigor
• Report discrepancies to improve system performance
• Involve senior colleagues when AI conflicts with clinical presentation

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Maintaining Diagnostic Skills

Subtitle: Preventing skill erosion from AI dependence

Content:
• Regular training keeps independent diagnostic abilities sharp
• Practice image interpretation without AI assistance
• Stay informed about AI limitations and updates
• Never stay silent when uncertain about AI outputs
• Treat AI as supportive tool, not definitive authority
