Title: When Helpful Becomes Harmful

Subtitle: The fine line AI must not cross

Content:
• AI tools in healthcare are becoming faster, cheaper, and more widely used
• Clinicians increasingly rely on AI suggestions for diagnosis and treatment
• But helpful-looking outputs can carry serious hidden risks
• Errors may be subtle, confident-sounding, and easy to miss
• Understanding where AI falls short is now a core clinical skill

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Pattern Recognition Without Understanding

Subtitle: Seeing patterns, missing clinical context

Content:
• Spots patterns, not causes
• Misses rare or complex cases
• No grasp of patient history
• Ignores clinical nuance
• Correlation confused with diagnosis

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Confidence Without Comprehension

Subtitle: How AI misleads with false certainty

Content:
• AI systems often state incorrect information in the same confident tone as correct information
• No internal alarm when the system is wrong
• A plausible-sounding answer is not the same as an accurate one
• Trained on patterns in data, not on genuine understanding of disease or patient context
• Clinicians may lower their guard simply because the output reads as authoritative
• Certainty in the wording does not reflect certainty in the evidence

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Bias Baked Into the Data

Subtitle: When training data skews the outcome

Content:
• Garbage in, garbage out
• Biased data, biased results
• Minorities often underrepresented
• Historical inequities get amplified
• Skewed outputs harm patients

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Blind Spots in AI Reasoning

Subtitle: Where AI reasoning silently goes wrong

Content:
• Trained on historical data, so past biases get quietly baked into outputs
• Confidently wrong
• No ability to sense what is missing from an incomplete patient history
• Rare conditions often underrepresented in training data, making them easy for AI to overlook
• Pattern-matching across populations, not reasoning about the individual in front of you
• No awareness of context, urgency, or the clinician's gut instinct

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Gaps in AI Clinical Reasoning

Subtitle: Where AI reasoning falls short clinically

Content:
• Unable to weigh up the full clinical picture the way an experienced clinician can
• Misses subtle cues
• No access to a patient's tone, body language, or lived history
• Struggles with rare or atypical presentations that fall outside its training data
• Cannot account for local protocols, resource availability, or patient values
• Pattern-matching is not the same as clinical judgement

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Red Flags to Watch For

Subtitle: Warning signs clinicians must recognise now

Content:
• AI sounds overly confident
• No source cited
• Rare conditions missed
• Contradicts your training
• Output never questioned

Title: What to Remember

Subtitle: Core principles for safer AI use

Content:
• AI assists, never decides
• Always verify AI output
• Patient safety comes first
• Escalate when uncertain
• You hold clinical responsibility
• Speak up, stay vigilant
