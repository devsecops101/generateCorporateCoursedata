Title: AI and Patient Data Privacy

Subtitle: Protecting sensitive health data with AI

Content:
• AI handles sensitive health data
• Risks are real and serious
• Privacy rules must be followed
• You play a key role

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: What Counts as Patient Data

Subtitle: More than just names and diagnoses

Content:
• Full names, dates of birth, and NHS numbers
• Home addresses, phone numbers, and email addresses
• Photos, voice recordings, or any biometric data
• Clinical notes, test results, diagnoses, and prescriptions
• Anything that could identify a patient when combined with other details
• Even appointment times and attendance records

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Risks of AI Data Misuse

Subtitle: When data falls into wrong hands

Content:
• Unauthorised access to AI systems can expose patient diagnoses, medications, and personal identifiers
• Data breaches affecting AI tools often involve large volumes of records at once
• Re-identification of anonymised data using AI pattern recognition
• Third-party AI vendors may store or process data outside NHS governance controls
• Leaked health data can be used for discrimination, fraud, or targeted scams

Slide 4 (Slide 4)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Legal and Regulatory Framework

Subtitle: Your obligations under data protection law

Content:
• UK GDPR requires explicit consent for processing health data
• Data Protection Act 2018 sets specific rules for NHS organisations
• ICO can impose significant fines for privacy breaches
• Common law duty of confidentiality applies to all patient information
• Professional bodies have clear guidance on data handling responsibilities

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Safe Tool Selection

Subtitle: Choosing approved AI platforms

Content:
• Use approved tools only
• Avoid general-purpose AI platforms for patient data
• Check your organisation's approved software list
• Verify data processing locations and storage policies
• Ensure proper contractual safeguards are in place

Slide 6 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Data Preparation Best Practices

Subtitle: Making data safe before AI processing

Content:
• Anonymise data before sharing
• Remove all direct identifiers first
• Consider indirect identifiers that could combine
• Use pseudonymisation where full anonymisation isn't possible
• Document your anonymisation methods

Slide 7 (Slide 7)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Access Control and Monitoring

Subtitle: Managing who sees what

Content:
• Limit access strictly to need-to-know basis
• Log every data interaction for audit trails
• Review permissions regularly as roles change
• Monitor for unusual access patterns
• Report breaches immediately when they occur

Slide 8 (Slide 8)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Points to Remember

Subtitle: Core principles for safe AI practice

Content:
• Never enter real patient names, dates of birth, or NHS numbers into any AI tool
• Anonymise first, always
• Even seemingly harmless details can combine to identify a patient
• Check your organisation's approved AI policy before using any new tool
• If in doubt, leave it out
• Speak to your information governance lead for guidance on safe next steps
