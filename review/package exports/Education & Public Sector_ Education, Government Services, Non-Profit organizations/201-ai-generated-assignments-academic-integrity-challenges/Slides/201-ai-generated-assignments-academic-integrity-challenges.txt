Title: AI and Academic Integrity Today

Subtitle: Where learning and AI now meet

Content:
• AI writing tools now widely available to students at every level
• Assignments submitted today may be fully or partly machine-generated
• Traditional plagiarism checks often miss AI-produced content
• Academic integrity policies struggling to keep pace
• A growing challenge for educators, institutions, and learners alike

Slide 2 (Slide 2)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Rise of AI Writing

Subtitle: From novelty to everyday student tool

Content:
• AI writing tools available to students for free, with no technical skill required
• Capable of producing full essays in seconds
• Adoption among students rising sharply since 2022
• Works across subjects, from history essays to science reports
• Many students now treat these tools as a normal part of their workflow
• Institutions largely unprepared for how quickly this shift happened

Slide 3 (Slide 3)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Defining AI-Generated Content

Subtitle: What counts as AI-generated work?

Content:
• Text, images, code, or essays produced fully or partly by an AI tool such as ChatGPT or Copilot
• Includes lightly edited AI output passed off as original student work
• Not just copied text
• Also covers AI-assisted paraphrasing, summarising, or restructuring of source material
• The line between using AI as a writing aid and submitting AI-generated work is often blurry

Slide 5 (Slide 5)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Rethinking Assessment in Practice

Subtitle: Designing assessments that AI cannot replace

Content:
• Shift focus from final products to the process of thinking and creating
• Oral assessments, live demonstrations, and reflective journals are harder to fake
• Personal, local, or time-bound tasks (such as analysing a recent campus event) resist generic AI responses
• Build in checkpoints
• Peer critique and iterative drafts reveal genuine student understanding over time
• Authentic tasks rooted in real experience give students something worth producing
