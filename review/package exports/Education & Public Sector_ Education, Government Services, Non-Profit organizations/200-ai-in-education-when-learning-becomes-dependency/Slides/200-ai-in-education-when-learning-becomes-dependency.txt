Title: The Hidden Cost of Shortcuts

Subtitle: Convenience now, capability lost later.

Content:
• Students who use AI to complete tasks miss the struggle that builds real understanding
• No productive struggle, no lasting experience
• Over time, gaps in foundational knowledge become harder to close
• Convenience feels like progress but often replaces it
• Core abilities like critical thinking, writing, and problem-solving quietly erode with each shortcut taken

Title: The Dependency Trap Explained

Subtitle: When shortcuts replace genuine understanding

Content:
• Students using AI to complete tasks without engaging in the thinking process behind them
• Repeated shortcuts that quietly erode problem-solving and critical reasoning skills
• The brain learns by struggling
• Dependency forms gradually, often before students (or teachers) notice anything is wrong
• Confidence built on AI output rather than genuine personal understanding
• A student who always borrows answers never discovers what they actually know
