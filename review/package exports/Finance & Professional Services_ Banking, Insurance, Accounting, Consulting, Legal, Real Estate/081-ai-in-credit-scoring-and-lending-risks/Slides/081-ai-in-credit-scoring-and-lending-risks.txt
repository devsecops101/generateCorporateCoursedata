Title: Regulatory Frameworks and Compliance

Subtitle: Navigating Compliance in AI Lending

Content:
• Key regulations shaping AI lending include the EU AI Act, the Equal Credit Opportunity Act, and the UK FCA's fairness guidelines
• Lenders must document how their AI models reach decisions to satisfy explainability requirements
• Compliance is not optional
• Regulators increasingly expect firms to test for bias before deploying credit scoring systems
• Audit trails and model governance records help demonstrate accountability during regulatory reviews

Slide 8 (Slide 6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Key Points to Remember

Subtitle: Core principles for responsible AI lending

Content:
• AI credit models must be regularly audited for bias to protect applicants from unfair outcomes
• Explainability is not optional
• Regulators in the UK and beyond increasingly require lenders to justify automated decisions in plain language
• Diverse, representative training data reduces the risk of discriminatory lending patterns
• Always question what a model cannot see, not just what it can
• As a next step, review your organisation's current lending processes to identify where AI decisions may need greater transparency or human oversight
